# User Guide — Missing Persons Flyer Generator

This guide is written for deputies, detectives, and officers who need to create a missing persons flyer during their shift. If you're looking for setup or deployment instructions, see [SETUP.md](SETUP.md) and [DEPLOY.md](DEPLOY.md) instead.

---

## Quick Start — Your First Flyer in 4 Steps

1. **Open the tool.** Double-click the bookmark on your desktop, or go to the URL your department gave you.
2. **Fill out the form** on the left side of the screen. At minimum: name, age, and last seen location.
3. **Upload a photo.** Click the upload box or drag a photo file onto it.
4. **Click PDF** at the bottom to download. Done.

You'll also get a `.json` backup file downloaded at the same time. Keep this in case you need to update the flyer later.

---

## Picking the Right Alert Type

Three buttons at the top of the form. Pick the right one — it changes the flyer's look and adds required fields.

| Type | Use when... |
|------|-------------|
| **Missing Person** | An adult or older teen is missing. No suspected abduction. |
| **AMBER Alert** | A child has been abducted and is in danger. Adds fields for suspect info. |
| **Silver Alert** | A missing adult has Alzheimer's, dementia, or another cognitive condition. Adds fields for medical info. |

Switching the alert type changes the flyer's colors (AMBER is black/orange, Silver is navy/silver) and reveals any extra fields that alert type needs.

---

## Filling Out the Form

Work top to bottom. The preview on the right updates as you type.

**Case Information**
- Report number — your agency's case number
- Submitted By — your name and badge (optional, shows small on the flyer)

**Identity**
- Full name, sex, race (dropdown), hair, eyes (type or pick from suggestions)
- Date of Birth auto-calculates age. Or just type the age directly.
- Height: type feet and inches in separate boxes

**Last Seen**
- Date and time (use the calendar picker)
- Location — be specific (street, cross-street, city)
- Clothing — what they were wearing
- Distinguishing features — tattoos, scars, jewelry, etc.
- Vehicle — if they left in one

**Narrative**
- A small callout box tells you what to include
- Keep it tight — 3 to 5 sentences is plenty
- Don't repeat info that's already in other fields

**Empty fields disappear from the flyer automatically.** You don't need to fill in every field — just what you know.

---

## Photos

- Upload 1 to 4 photos. First photo is the big one. Others appear as thumbnails.
- **To reposition**: click and drag the main photo in the preview to move the subject's face where you want it.
- **To zoom in**: use the Zoom slider under the upload box.
- **To start over**: click Reset.
- Photos are auto-resized to save space. Upload whatever size you have — the tool handles it.

---

## Exporting Your Flyer

Four buttons at the bottom:

| Button | What it does |
|--------|-------------|
| **PDF** | Downloads a print-ready PDF (also saves a `.json` backup) |
| **PNG** | Downloads an image file (good for email attachments) |
| **Copy for Social** | Copies a ready-to-paste social media post to your clipboard |
| **Print** | Opens the print dialog |

**Format dropdown** lets you pick the size:
- **Letter (8.5×11")** — standard print size
- **Instagram (4:5)** — vertical, good for social media feeds
- **Square (1:1)** — good for Instagram posts and sharing

**Keyboard shortcuts:** `Ctrl+S` for PDF, `Ctrl+Shift+S` for PNG, `Ctrl+P` for print.

---

## Updating a Flyer Later

If the subject is located, or new information comes in, you can reload the flyer and update it.

1. Click **Edit an Existing Flyer** at the top of the page
2. Select the saved PDF or `.json` backup file
3. All fields, photos, and settings reload
4. Make your changes
5. Export a new version

**If the subject is found:** check **Mark Located** at the bottom. A green "LOCATED" banner appears across the flyer. Export and share.

**You can also drag the PDF or JSON file** anywhere on the page to load it.

---

## Copy for Social Media

Click **Copy for Social** and the tool puts a complete post on your clipboard. Paste it into Facebook, X, Nextdoor, or anywhere else. Looks like this:

```
🚨 MISSING PERSON 🚨

Alex J. Sample · 34 · Male · White
Last seen: 400 Block of Main Street at October 20, 2026 6:30 PM
Wearing: Gray hoodie, dark jeans, black sneakers

[Narrative appears here]

🚔 If seen right now: 911
📞 With information: (555) 123-4567
```

## What to Do When...

**...I lose my work or the browser crashes.**
The tool auto-saves every few seconds. Reopen the file — your draft should be there. If photos are missing, it's because they were too large. Export to PDF immediately when you have what you need.

**...I need to edit a flyer another deputy made.**
Ask them to send you the PDF or `.json` backup. Drag it into the tool. All fields load.

**...I need to start over on a new case.**
Click **Clear Form** at the top. This wipes everything and starts fresh.

**...someone else used the MDC before me.**
You might see their draft when you open the tool. Click **Clear Form** and start clean.

**...the subject is found.**
Reload the old flyer (Edit an Existing Flyer), check **Mark Located**, re-export, and share the updated version.

**...I want to see how the tool works before using it for a real case.**
Click **Try a Demo** at the top. It fills in sample data with a placeholder photo so you can see everything in action.

---

## Tips from Experience

- **Export early, export often.** Even if you're not done, export a draft PDF. It's free insurance.
- **Keep the `.json` backup file.** It's the only way to guarantee you can edit the flyer later.
- **Be specific in the narrative.** "Last seen at McDonald's on Main Street at 4 PM" is better than "left home in the afternoon."
- **Use the right alert type.** AMBER is reserved for confirmed child abductions. Silver is for cognitively impaired adults. Using them wrong can dilute the system's credibility.
- **Don't use this tool for anything other than missing persons.** It's purpose-built.

# Printable One-Page Cheat Sheet

> **Print this page and tape it to your MDC.**

## Missing Persons Flyer Generator — Quick Reference

**1. Pick Alert Type** → Missing Person, AMBER (child abduction), or Silver (cognitive impairment)

**2. Fill the Form (top to bottom)**
- Report number, your name
- Identity: name, age, sex, race, height, weight, hair, eyes
- Last seen: when, where, clothing, distinguishing features
- Narrative: 3-5 sentences

**3. Upload a Photo**
- Click the upload box or drag a photo in
- Drag the main photo to reposition
- Use the Zoom slider to zoom in

**4. Export**
- **PDF** (`Ctrl+S`) — for printing and sharing
- **PNG** (`Ctrl+Shift+S`) — for social media attachments
- **Copy for Social** — ready-to-paste post
- **Print** (`Ctrl+P`) — direct print

## Editing an Existing Flyer
Click **Edit an Existing Flyer** → pick the PDF or `.json` backup → update → re-export.

## If Subject is Located
Reload the flyer → check **Mark Located** → re-export → share.

## Golden Rules
- Always save the `.json` backup file with the PDF
- Click **Clear Form** when done (especially on shared MDCs)
- Empty fields hide from the flyer automatically
- Export early and often

## When Something Goes Wrong
- Lost work? Reopen the file — auto-save restores it.
- Photo too large? Export to PDF immediately.
- Seeing someone else's draft? Click Clear Form.

## Need Help?
Demo mode: click **Try a Demo** at the top of the page to see how it works with sample data.
